Who we are

A family of Pashtuns who found one another on Long Island — and built a home for our culture here.

The Pashtuns Society of Long Island began the way most good things do — with a few families who missed home, and decided to build a piece of it here.

We come from different cities and valleys, but we share one language, one table, and one idea of what it means to be a good neighbor. PSOLI is a registered nonprofit cultural society — nonpolitical and nonreligious — open to every Pashtun family on Long Island and the friends who stand with us.

Our work is simple: bring people together, look after one another, and make sure our children grow up knowing the poetry, the food, the music, and the manners that make us who we are.

What we stand for

UnityOne community that shows up for each other — in celebration and in hardship.
CultureKeeping Pashto, hospitality, and our traditions alive for the next generation.
CommunityA welcoming home for every family, and warm hospitality for every guest.
